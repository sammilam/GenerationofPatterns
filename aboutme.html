About Me

Hi, I'm Sammi,
and this is my year-long thesis!

I’ve always admired patterns. Visually, they are captivating—one motif repeating itself across a surface, creating harmony and rhythm. Designing patterns can be deceptively simple: a single square repeated to form something greater. Patterns are everywhere, embedded in our surroundings and behaviour. Humans instinctively connect the dots, using patterns to process information, navigate challenges, and create meaning.

But why do we like patterns? Perhaps they bring a sense of order and organization, offering familiarity and comfort. Repetition allows us to move through life effortlessly, building a foundation for new opportunities. Yet, patterns are not always beneficial. History, too, repeats itself—sometimes with devastating consequences. As the Earth warms and conflicts persist, we find ourselves trapped in cycles of chaos.

How might patterns offer a perspective? An element of surprise? What is seen normal that probably should not be overlooked? Our design process mirrors life: research, repeat, and restate. By studying and understanding our experiences, we can disrupt harmful patterns and create meaningful change. Patterns, while often seen as static, hold the potential to inspire growth, transformation, and unlimited possibilities.

Sammi Lam (she/her)

Based in Toronto, Sammi Lam is a passionate and curious graphic designer who specializes in motion, web and brand design. She loves playing with various mediums and embracing constraints to produce new ways of design thinking. Through her thesis, she aims to uncover greater truths that open up unconventional possibilities and ideas that question norms and create meaningful conversations.

Behind the Scenes

In case you weren't there, I hosted a raffle during our Fall Semester term exhibition. I have built a website documenting my daily generative art inspired by my strongest emotion per day. I hoped to take this opportunity to survey the public and collect opinions on the relationship between mood and patterns. I thought of a main component. An “infinite scroll” of patterns in a rolodex to project live changes. This was a physical depiction of what I intended to code for my final website. An abandoned website can be found here!

Fall Semester Generative Art

In the end, it is a current artifact signifying the past work I have done. It took a lot of manual labour to cut, glue, and assemble the cards. I thought the process of repetition was ironic to the idea of patterns. Additionally, the collected results turned into my book: Pattern Profiles. If you have entered the raffle, please check out your pattern! I thought it would be fun to see everyone's patterns in a book.
